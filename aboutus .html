 Take The Walk Today For A Better Tomorrow

.....Empowering helpless

Dont Stop, Dont Look Back, Dont Think Once More, Because enough is enough

The purpose of Take The Walks is not to supplant the governmental relief agencies but to act as a coordinating mechanism between the government apparatus and the affected populace. There was however felt, during the kedarnath tragedy of 2013, a lack of an advised and coherent approach in the way these organizations operated. Consequently the government has undertaken to initiate steps to better coordinate the working of NGOs vis-à-vis disaster management. An NGO named Karmayog, which is basically a cost-free portal, was set up to provide an easy means of data and communication exchange between the MCGM, NGOs and among NGOs themselves.

Take The Walk is a major Non Governmental Organization (NGO) in BVM in terms of amount of information it can circulate. Take The Walk is dedicated to the study and training of volunteer workers in Disaster Management, Child Relife,People Help,Poor Assistance since its inception in 2013. Take The Walk is a purely voluntary, non profit organization found and run by the ASHA Trust, it’s run as a part of the Trust’s Online service.

Take The Walk has its central command centre located at BVM . It is largely self funded, though it’s open to all voluneers. Each of the city’s 10 wards is treated as a zone which is further divided into local micro-zones. Each micro-zone has its own team of volunteers. The micro zones are marked in such a way that communication between the volunteers is possible at any given moment. Take The Walk claims to have 50 volunteers and 2 centres spread across Nainital. Typically these centres interface with the Ward Officer and at any time 10 volunteers are in contact with the ward office. Another 5 volunteers are on stand by for emergency assignment. Each center is equipped with a number of computers to spread out information as fast as possible. Take The Walk helps the school administration by providing extreme database and information center which lacks in other NGOs.
